# Guide: Signals

The **Signals** page is LuxQuant's live signal terminal. It shows trading
signals generated by the LuxQuant algorithm, a market-direction snapshot for Bitcoin,
performance stats, and a detailed proof/analysis modal for each signal. This document
explains everything on the page and inside each signal.

---

## 1. Page header & stats

At the top you'll see a summary line and stat cards:

- **LAST 7 DAYS · N SIGNALS · N RECENTLY UPDATED** — how many signals exist in the
  current window and how many were updated recently.
- **UPDATED hh:mm** — the last time the data refreshed (data auto-refreshes).
- **Today's Activity** — number of signals called today, with a breakdown of Open /
  Wins (W) / Losses (L).
- **Today's Win Rate** — percentage of today's *closed* signals that were wins.
- **Overall Win Rate** — historical win rate across all signals, plus the total number
  of signals ever generated.

---

## 2. BTC Compass (24h market read)

A snapshot panel that summarizes Bitcoin's short-term outlook, because most altcoins
move with BTC. It shows:

- **Bias + confidence** — e.g. `NEUTRAL 43%`, `BULLISH`, or `BEARISH`, with a
  confidence percentage.
- **Invalidation / Live / Target** — a price track: the current (Live) BTC price,
  the **Target** it's heading toward, and the **Invalidation** level where the read
  breaks (with % distance to each).
- **What this means** — a plain-language summary of the 24h read.
- **What's driving it (24h drivers)** — three inputs, each Bullish or Bearish:
  - **Price Action** — recent price behaviour.
  - **Derivatives** — funding rate and futures positioning.
  - **Liquidation Liquidity** — where liquidation clusters sit (upside/downside).
- **Alt Exposure — how to trade the signals below** — guidance like "Range only"
  (trade level-to-level) vs trending conditions.
- **Full Outlook** and **Projection Chart** buttons open deeper BTC views.

## 3. Coin Flow Intensity

A section (tap to expand) showing where money is currently flowing across coins —
useful to spot which assets are attracting capital right now.

## 4. Call Filter (finding & filtering signals)

- **Date tabs** — Watchlist, All Days, Today, Yesterday, or a specific date. The
  number next to each label is the count of signals for that day.
- **Search pair** — token-based search. Type `BTC` to match everything starting with
  BTC, or `BTCUSDT` for an exact pair match.
- **Called Time / order** — sorting (newest/oldest, etc.).
- **Advanced Filters** (expandable):
  - **Status** — open / updated / outcome.
  - **Risk** — NORMAL or HIGH.
  - **Intelligence Filters** — *Hot streak* (strong recent win run) and **Verdict**
    (*Worth It* / *Avoid*).
  - **BTC correlation** — *Decoupled* (independent of BTC) or *High align* (moves
    strongly with BTC).
  - **Pattern Filters** — technical pattern tags (e.g. `fvg near entry`,
    `rsi overbought`, `bb expansion`, `vol climax`, `liq very low`, `btc bullish`).
- **Reset All** — clears every active filter.
- **Columns 12/12** — toggle which table columns are visible.
- **Watchlist star** — the star on each row saves a signal to your personal watchlist.

## 5. Signals Table columns

- **Pair** — the coin pair, e.g. `BTCUSDT` (BTC priced in USDT).
- **Price** — current price with % change.
- **Entry** — the suggested entry price.
- **Target** — take-profit target(s), TP1–TP4; green shows distance from entry.
- **Stop Loss (SL)** — the risk-limit price; if hit, the signal closes at a loss.
- **Risk** — `NORMAL` or `HIGH` (HIGH = more volatile / higher risk).
- **MCap** — market capitalization (size; small = small-cap).
- **Vol 24H** — 24-hour trading volume (liquidity).
- **WR / Streak** — **Win Rate** (historical % of this pattern's signals that reached
  target) and **Streak** (recent run: ▲2W = 2 wins in a row, ▼1L = 1 loss). WR is
  color-coded: green ≥70%, yellow 50–69%, red below 50%.
- **BTC Corr** — correlation to Bitcoin. **ρ (rho)** = how closely it moves with BTC
  (−1 to +1); **β (beta)** = how strongly (β>1 means it amplifies BTC moves).
  "Decoupled" = moving independently of BTC.
- **Verdict** — Coin Intelligence assessment: `WORTH IT` (with score) or an avoid
  marker. See section 8.
- **Status** — `OPEN` (running), `TP1`/`TP2`/`TP3`/`TP4` (nth target hit), `WIN`
  (closed in profit), `LOSS` (stop loss hit).
- **Called Time** — when the signal was called, plus "x hours ago".

Click any row to open the **signal detail modal**.

## 6. Signal detail modal

Opening a signal shows a full workspace with a header, tabs, a trade journey, and
tools.

### Header
Shows the pair, **status** badge (WIN/LOSS/TP/OPEN), **risk** badge, coin tags (e.g.
`Layer 1`, `Has Utility`), the **BTC score** (correlation strength with ρ/β), the
signal's **called time**, and buttons for **Indicator guide** and **Deep Analysis**.

### Tabs
- **Chart** — a live TradingView chart of the pair. You can toggle **indicators**
  (MACD · RSI · BB / Bollinger Bands) on/off. "How to use indicator" opens a guide.
- **Trade** — the **Trade Proof**: a BEFORE (entry) chart and an AFTER (result) chart
  side by side, plus **Entry price**, **Last price**, and **Highest Price After
  Called** (peak). This is the visual proof of how the call played out. There's also a
  "Copy to AI" action that exports the trade as a prompt.
- **Research** — fundamental coin info pulled from CoinGecko (about the project,
  market data, links).
- **History** — the full timeline of updates for this signal (each TP/SL event with
  time and price).

### Signal Journey
A vertical timeline of the trade's stages: **SL → Entry → TP1 → TP2 → TP3 → TP4**.
Each stage shows whether it was reached, the time, price, and % from entry. The
connecting rail brightens as later stages are reached.

### Deep Analysis (the "Deep Analysis" button)
Opens a facts-and-tags breakdown of the coin. Its philosophy is **"inform, don't
decide"** — it shows raw facts with descriptive tags, deliberately **no single
confidence score or buy/sell rating**. Features:
- **Timeframe tabs**: ALL, M15, H1, H4, and CONTEXT — the same signal viewed across
  15-minute, 1-hour, and 4-hour perspectives plus broader context.
- An at-a-glance summary band (entry snapshot + live snapshot).
- **Copy to AI**: export the analysis as **Markdown**, **JSON**, or a ready-made
  **AI Prompt** so you can paste it into an external AI tool for your own research.

### Trade on Exchanges
Buttons/links to open or trade the pair on supported exchanges (and the original
Telegram message link where available).

## 7. Statuses & outcomes (what the badges mean)

- **OPEN** — the signal is live and running; no target or stop hit yet.
- **TP1 / TP2 / TP3 / TP4** — the 1st–4th take-profit target has been reached.
  Higher TP = the move ran further in profit.
- **WIN** — the signal closed in profit.
- **LOSS** — the price hit the stop loss; the signal closed at a loss.

---

## 8. Coin Intelligence metrics (Verdict, Score, and more)

LuxQuant scores each coin's signal history. These appear in the Verdict column and in
Deep Analysis.

- **Verdict** — a simple label:
  - **Worth It** — generally when the win rate is strong (about ≥80% with enough
    closed trades, or ≥85% overall), or the coin is on a strong hot streak.
  - **Avoid** — when the win rate is weak (below ~65% with enough closed trades),
    the **SL Rate is high (≥30%)**, or distribution/underperformance flags are present.
  - **Neutral** — not clearly in either bucket.
- **Risk Score (0–100)** with a grade: **Excellent ≥80, Good ≥65, Average ≥45,
  Poor ≥25, Very Poor** below 25.
- **SL Rate** — the share of this coin's trades that ended at stop loss (lower is
  better; ≥30% is a warning).
- **Streak** — current run of wins (W) or losses (L).
- **R:R Ratio** — reward-to-risk ratio. ≥2x is strong, ≥1x acceptable, below 1x weak.
- **Entry Quality** — how good the entry zone tends to be, including "Hits > TP1"
  (how often price runs past TP1) and "Full Target Rate" (how often it reaches the
  final target).
- **TP4 streaks** — total TP4 hits, longest streak, and current streak.

## 9. Finding promising small-cap coins

Combine filters: look for a small **MCap**, make sure **Vol 24H** is healthy
(liquidity), then reinforce with **Verdict = Worth It** and/or **Hot streak**. Small
caps are often **HIGH** risk, so check the Risk column and use the BTC Compass read to
judge overall market conditions.

## 10. Quick glossary

- **TP1–TP4** = Take Profit levels 1–4 (staged profit targets).
- **SL / SL1 / SL2** = Stop Loss (risk limits).
- **WR** = Win Rate. **Streak** = run of wins/losses.
- **ρ (rho)** = correlation with BTC; **β (beta)** = strength/amplitude vs BTC.
- **Decoupled** = moving independently of Bitcoin.
- **FVG** = Fair Value Gap; **RSI** = Relative Strength Index; **BB** = Bollinger
  Bands; **MACD** = Moving Average Convergence Divergence.
- **R:R** = Reward-to-Risk ratio.

## Important note

Everything on this page — signals, scores, verdicts, and analysis — is **information
and tooling**, not a recommendation to buy or sell. All trading decisions are the
user's own.

## Comparing several calls (Compare)

Every row has a checkbox on the far left. Tick 2–5 calls and a bar appears at the
bottom of the screen; press **Compare** to put them side by side.

**Everything in Compare is measured from the LIVE price, not the original entry.**
That is deliberate. The planned risk/reward — target minus entry, over entry minus
stop — is roughly 4.5 on *every* call, because the algorithm sets targets and stops
at fixed ratios. Comparing on that number would be comparing rounding noise. What
actually differs between two calls is how far price has already travelled, so the
headline row is **R:R from here**: the upside still left to the target divided by
the distance down to the stop, both from the current price.

- Above 1× — you stand to make more than you risk by entering now.
- Below 1× — the remaining upside is smaller than the downside to the stop.
- "below stop" — price has already broken the invalidation level; it is not a trade.

Other rows: room to target, risk to stop, distance from the original entry (near
zero means you can still get in at the called price), live price, entry/stop,
target, 24h liquidity, BTC correlation and age. The best value in each row is
highlighted.

At the top, a **verdict** names the strongest of the selection and says why — and
says plainly when none of them is worth taking rather than inventing a winner.

Compare also warns about **concentration**: if the coins you picked all track BTC
closely (correlation ≥ 0.70), taking several of them is one larger BTC bet rather
than a spread of risk. That is something no single-signal view can tell you.
