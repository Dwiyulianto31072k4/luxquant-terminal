# Guide: Money Flow

The Money Flow page shows where capital is moving across the crypto market — which
sectors and coins are seeing buying vs selling, plus large ("whale") transactions. It
has three tabs.

> Note: This is market information, not a recommendation to buy or sell.

## Macro gauges (top)

Quick market-wide readings, for example:
- **Stablecoin Dominance** — stablecoins' share of total market cap, with its 7-day
  change (in percentage points, "pp"). Rising stablecoin dominance = money sitting on
  the sidelines (risk-off); falling = capital rotating into coins (risk-on).

## Tab 1 — Sectors

**Sector Rotation** — a ranked list of crypto sectors (Layer 1, DeFi, AI, memes, etc.)
by how their combined market cap is changing:
- **24h / 7d change** — sector momentum over those windows.
- **Market Cap** — the sector's size.
- The leading sector is highlighted. This shows which narratives money is rotating
  into or out of.

## Tab 2 — Coins

A filterable **flow table** for individual coins. Each coin gets a flow label:
- **Net Buying** — more buying pressure than selling.
- **Net Selling** — more selling pressure than buying.
- **Balanced** — roughly even.

Other details:
- **Intensity bars** show how strong the flow is.
- A desktop 2-column view aligns **spot Flow** next to **DEX** flow.
- Coins that currently have a LuxQuant signal ("calls") are highlighted; clicking a
  call row opens that signal's detail modal.

## Tab 3 — Whale

A live feed of large ("whale") transactions — big transfers and trades that can move
markets. Useful to spot when large players are accumulating or offloading.

## How to use it

Check the **macro gauges** for risk-on vs risk-off, use **Sectors** to see which
narratives are rotating in, then drill into **Coins** to find where buying/selling
pressure is concentrated — and cross-reference highlighted calls with signals. The
**Whale** feed adds context on big-player activity.

## Important note

Money Flow is information to help you read the market — not financial advice. Trading
decisions are your own.
