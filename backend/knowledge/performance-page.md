# Guide: Performance Hub

The Performance Hub is LuxQuant's transparent track record — it shows how the signals
have actually performed over time and breaks the results down many ways. It has three
views, each with its own sub-tabs.

> Note: Past performance is historical information, not a promise of future results,
> and not financial advice.

## Views & sub-tabs

**All-Time** — the overall track record across the entire history (headline win rate
and totals).

**Daily** — recent, day-level performance with sub-tabs:
- **Overview** — win rate, hit rate, and resolved-signal summary for the period.
- **By Pattern** — performance grouped by technical pattern tag (which setups work
  best).
- **Correlation** — how results relate to BTC / market conditions.
- **By Sector** — performance grouped by coin sector.
- **Today's Edge** — the day's standout read / best current opportunity context.

**Research (Edge Lab)** — deeper analytics with sub-tabs:
- **Calibration** — whether the model's confidence matches real outcomes (are "high
  confidence" signals actually winning more?).
- **Pattern × BTC** — how each pattern performs under different BTC regimes.
- **Expected Value (EV)** — the average expected outcome per signal, combining win
  rate and average win/loss size.
- **Calendar** — performance by date/time.
- **Timing** — how results vary by time of day / entry timing.
- **Coins** — per-coin performance breakdown.
- **WR × BTC** — win rate mapped against BTC behaviour.

## Key metrics

- **Win Rate (WR)** — the percentage of resolved signals that were wins. Color-coded:
  green ≥75% (or ≥70% in some views), neutral 50–74%, red below 50%.
- **Hit Rate** — wins over total (shown as `wins/total` plus a percentage).
- **Resolved signals** — signals that have closed (win or loss); open ones aren't
  counted in win rate.
- **Expected Value (EV)** — average result per trade; positive EV means the edge is
  net-profitable over many trades even if individual trades lose.
- **Calibration** — alignment between predicted confidence and actual win rate.
- **Small-sample badge** — a "⚠ small sample" marker appears when there aren't enough
  signals (e.g. n < 15) for a stat to be reliable. Treat those numbers with caution.

## How to use it

Start with **All-Time** for the headline record, use **Daily → By Pattern / By Sector**
to see what's working now, and use **Research** (Calibration, EV) to judge how
trustworthy and profitable the edge is. Always check the sample size before trusting a
breakdown.

## Important note

These are historical statistics to help you understand the system's track record. They
are not a guarantee of future results and not financial advice.
