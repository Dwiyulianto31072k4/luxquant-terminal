# Guide: Delistings

The Delistings page tracks coins being removed (delisted) from major exchanges. A
delisting is a major risk event — the token often drops sharply — so this page helps
you avoid or react to them. This guide explains it.

> Note: This is risk information, not a recommendation to buy or sell.

## What it shows

A sortable, filterable table of delisting announcements per token, including:
- **Coin / token** being delisted.
- **Exchange** announcing it (Binance, Bybit, OKX, and others).
- **Announced** date and the **delist date/deadline** (when trading stops).
- **Peak** — how the price moved around the event.
- A marker when **LuxQuant called** a trade after the delisting announcement.

## Filters & sorting

- **Tabs** — filter by exchange (All, Binance, Bybit, OKX, …).
- **Search** — find a specific token.
- **Sort** — by delist date, announced date, or peak.

## How to use it

Check whether any coin you hold or are watching is on the list, and note its **delist
deadline** — delisted tokens usually fall and can become hard to sell after the
deadline. Treat a delisting as a serious warning.

## Important note

Delisting data is a risk alert, not financial advice. Act on your own judgment.
