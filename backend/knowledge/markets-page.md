# Guide: Markets

The Markets page is a broad overview of the whole crypto market — global stats, a
heatmap, trending coins, sector performance, a coin table, and derivatives data. This
guide explains each section.

> Note: This page is data and information, not a recommendation to buy or sell.

## Global stats

- **Total Market Cap** — the combined value of all cryptocurrencies.
- **24h Volume** — total trading volume across the market in the last 24 hours
  (a liquidity / activity gauge).

## Heatmap

A grid of the **top ~50 coins by market cap**, sized/coloured by 24h performance —
green for gainers, red for losers. A fast way to see where money moved today and
whether the market is broadly up or down.

## Trending

The **most-searched** coins right now (attention/hype indicator). High search interest
doesn't mean a coin is good — just that people are looking at it.

## Top Categories

**Sector performance** — how different crypto categories (e.g. Layer 1, DeFi, AI,
memes) are doing. Useful to spot which narratives/sectors are rotating in or out.

## Coin table

A sortable list of coins with tabs:
- **All** — the default list.
- **Gainers** — sorted by biggest 24h price increase.
- **Losers** — sorted by biggest 24h drop.
- **Volume** — sorted by highest 24h trading volume.

## Derivatives & Liquidations

- **Derivatives (funding sentiment)** — the funding rate reflects whether futures
  traders are crowded long (positive) or short (negative).
- **Liquidations** — recently force-closed leveraged positions. Large liquidations
  often accompany sharp moves (longs liquidated on drops, shorts on rallies).

## Stablecoins & ETF flows

- **Stablecoins** — total stablecoin supply; growth can signal fresh capital ready to
  enter the market ("dry powder").
- **ETF Flows** — net money into or out of spot Bitcoin/Ethereum ETFs. Positive net
  flow = institutional buying; negative = outflows.

## Crypto News

A feed of recent market news for quick context on what's driving prices.

## How to use it

Scan the **heatmap** for the day's tone, check **categories** for sector rotation, use
the **coin table** tabs to find gainers/losers/volume, and read **derivatives, ETF
flows, and stablecoins** for positioning and capital context.

## Important note

Everything here is market information, not financial advice. Trading decisions are
your own.
