# Guide: Journal

The Journal is your personal trading diary. You log each trade — the plan, the
outcome, your emotions, and any mistakes — so you can review and improve over time.
This guide explains how to use it.

> Note: The Journal is a self-improvement tool. It does not give trading advice.

## Tabs

- **History** — your list of logged trades, with filters and sorting.
- **Entry** — the form to add or edit a trade entry.
- **Analytics** — charts and stats over your logged trades: win rate, an equity curve,
  a calendar heatmap, and insights into your patterns (best strategies, common
  mistakes, emotional trends).

## Logging a trade (the entry form)

- **Planned vs Actual** — record your **planned entry/exit** and your **actual
  entry/exit**, plus **size**. Comparing planned vs actual reveals discipline gaps.
- **P&L / % / R:R** — the journal auto-calculates profit/loss, percent return, and the
  reward-to-risk ratio from your numbers, including fees.
- **Strategy tags** — label the setup: LuxQuant Signal, Breakout, ICT / SMC, Mean
  Reversion, Scalp, Swing.
- **Confluence tags** — the reasons/confirmations behind the trade.
- **Mistake tags** — flag what went wrong: Early Exit, Moved SL, Oversized, FOMO Entry,
  Revenge Trade, No Plan, Ignored SL, Added to Loser. Tracking these is the fastest way
  to fix leaks.
- **Emotions** — rate confidence, FOMO level, mood, and regret. Emotional patterns
  often explain results more than the setup does.
- **Notes** — free-text reflection on the trade.

## Linking from signals

A LuxQuant signal can be sent straight into a new journal entry — it pre-fills the
pair, planned entry, and tags it as a "LuxQuant Signal" so you can track how you traded
the calls.

## Reviewing (History tab)

- Filter by strategy and sort by date or outcome.
- Each entry shows its status (e.g. WIN) and P&L.
- Review your **mistake tags** and **emotions** over time to spot recurring patterns.

## How to get value from it

Log every trade honestly — especially the losers and the mistakes. The point isn't a
pretty record; it's to find your repeated errors (moving stops, FOMO entries,
oversizing) and fix them.

## Note

The Journal helps you review your own trading. It is not financial advice.
