# Guide: Bitcoin

The Bitcoin page is a data dashboard for BTC — price, market structure, sentiment,
technicals, network health, and on-chain metrics in one place. This guide explains
each section and metric.

> Note: This page is data and information, not a recommendation to buy or sell.

## Price & market structure

- **Price** — the live BTC price with its 24h change.
- **Market Cap** — total value of all circulating BTC (price × circulating supply).
- **BTC Dominance** — BTC's share of the entire crypto market cap (%). Rising
  dominance means money favouring BTC over altcoins; falling can mean "alt season".
- **Circulating / Max Supply** — how many BTC exist now vs the 21M cap.
- **All-Time High (ATH)** — the highest price ever, and how far below it price is now.

## Fear & Greed Index

A market sentiment gauge from **0 to 100**:
- Low (0–25) = **Extreme Fear**, higher (75–100) = **Extreme Greed**.
- It blends volatility, momentum, volume, and social signals. Extreme fear can mark
  bottoms and extreme greed can mark local tops — but it's a mood indicator, not a
  timing tool.

## RSI (14)

**Relative Strength Index** over 14 periods, shown across timeframes:
- **≥ 70 = overbought** (price stretched up), **≤ 30 = oversold** (stretched down).
- Values between are neutral. RSI measures momentum, not certainty of reversal.

## Network health

- **Hashrate** — total mining power securing the network (higher = more secure).
- **Difficulty** — how hard it is to mine a block; adjusts to keep ~10-min blocks.
- **Mempool** — pending unconfirmed transactions waiting to be included.
- **Fees** — current transaction fee levels (rise when the network is busy).

## On-chain metrics

- **MVRV** — Market Value to Realized Value. Compares price to the average price coins
  last moved at. High MVRV can mean holders are in large profit (potential distribution
  risk); low/below 1 can mean the market is undervalued on-chain terms.
- Other on-chain readings describe holder behaviour and valuation.

## Derivatives sentiment

- **Funding rate** — periodic payment between long and short futures traders. Positive
  = longs pay shorts (bullish crowd positioning); negative = shorts pay longs.
- **Long/Short ratio** — the balance of traders positioned long vs short.

## How to use it

Read price + dominance for market structure, Fear & Greed for sentiment, RSI for
momentum stretch, and on-chain/derivatives for deeper context. Combine them rather
than relying on any single metric.

## Important note

Everything here is information to help you understand Bitcoin's market — not financial
advice. Trading decisions are your own.
