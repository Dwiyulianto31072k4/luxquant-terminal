# Guide: Order Book

The Order Book page shows the live depth of buy and sell orders for a pair (e.g.
BTC/USDT, ETH/USDT), so you can see where liquidity sits and how balanced the market
is. This guide explains it.

> Note: This is market microstructure information, not a recommendation to buy or sell.

## Key concepts

- **Bids** — buy orders (green). The total bid value shows demand waiting below price.
- **Asks** — sell orders (red). The total ask value shows supply waiting above price.
- **Imbalance** — the balance between bids and asks (bid % vs ask %). Heavier bids can
  indicate buy-side pressure; heavier asks, sell-side pressure.
- **Depth chart** — a visual of how much volume is stacked at each price level on both
  sides.
- **Spread** — the gap between the best bid and best ask (tighter = more liquid).
- **Walls** — unusually large orders at a price level that can act as support
  (bid wall) or resistance (ask wall).

## How to use it

Check the **imbalance** for short-term pressure, watch for **walls** near price (they
can slow or reverse moves), and use the **spread/depth** to judge liquidity before
trading. Order books change fast and large orders can be pulled.

## Important note

Order book data is real-time market structure, not financial advice. Trading decisions
are your own.
